Scenario Brief
Read before starting — Group
▲

You are the security leadership team of a mid-large enterprise within the Blackstone portfolio. Your organisation relies on a portfolio of third-party vendors for critical business functions — IT infrastructure, data processing, HR systems, payments and logistics.

Your vendor management programme runs annual questionnaire assessments and tracks certification status. This is what your programme has visibility of. External threat intelligence, dark web monitoring and financial distress signals are not currently in scope — you can see they exist as a capability, but the data is locked.

Your task: You have 8 minutes. Review your vendor portfolio below and select your top 3 highest-risk vendors — the ones you believe represent the greatest threat to your organisation right now. Click a vendor card to add it to your ranking.

▸ Locked signal categories are visible on each vendor card — but the data inside them is redacted. This is your programme's current blind spot.
▸ Base your decisions on what you can see. Your ranking will be compared against reality when the intelligence layer unlocks.

Key Takeaways

01

Questionnaires measure intent, not posture

NexaLink scored 87/100 on your assessment. They were ISO 27001 certified. Their credentials were being sold on dark web forums for 6 weeks. The questionnaire didn't lie — it just didn't look at the right things.

02

Nth-party risk is your risk

NexaLink's breach originated via a subsidiary in a high-risk jurisdiction — one that never appeared on your vendor intake form. Your contract was with NexaLink. The attack came through a company you'd never heard of.

03

The 24-hour NIS2 clock starts before you have answers

You had to choose between notifying the regulator with incomplete data or waiting for clarity and potentially breaching the notification window. Neither option was clean. That's not a process failure — it's the reality.

04

Portfolio-level risk is a shared problem

Three other Blackstone portfolio companies use NexaLink. By the time you isolated them, two had already been notified by the vendor's PR team rather than through an intel-sharing channel. The ecosystem is your extended attack surface.
